## Why Kratom Gummies Demand the Highest Vendor Standards

Gummies are the most technically demanding kratom product to manufacture correctly.

### The Heat Degradation Problem

Kratom alkaloids — particularly mitragynine and 7-hydroxymitragynine — are sensitive to heat. Standard gummy manufacturing involves heating the base mixture to **70–90°C** to achieve proper gelatin or pectin dissolution. At those temperatures, unprotected kratom alkaloids begin degrading.

Professional gummy manufacturers solve this by using stabilized kratom extracts that withstand the processing temperature and by adding the extract at the lowest possible temperature in the production cycle. Amateur or low-quality manufacturers skip these steps — producing gummies with dramatically lower alkaloid content than the label claims.

### The Dosing Accuracy Problem

Each gummy must contain a precise, consistent quantity of kratom extract. Achieving that consistency requires pharmaceutical-grade mixing equipment that distributes the extract homogeneously throughout the gummy mixture. Without it, one gummy might contain twice the extract of the next one in the same batch.

A 2021 FDA laboratory sampling program found **significant dosing inconsistency** in edible-format botanical products — with individual unit variance of up to 40% within a single batch from vendors without proper mixing equipment.

### Why COA Testing After Production Is Non-Negotiable

Unlike powder — where the product is the tested material — gummies require testing of the finished product after all manufacturing steps are complete. A COA on the raw kratom extract used in production tells you nothing about the alkaloid content of the finished gummy after heat processing, mixing, and setting.

The vendors below test finished gummy products. Not just their raw ingredients. That distinction is the difference between meaningful quality assurance and marketing documentation.

## How to Verify Kratom Gummy Quality Before Buying

Gummy verification requires specific checks beyond standard powder COA review.

### Verify Finished Product Testing — Not Raw Material Only

The most important gummy-specific verification step. Confirm that the COA provided is for the finished gummy product — not the raw kratom extract used in production. The COA document should reference the gummy product specifically — not just the extract batch.

Raw material COAs tell you the extract was clean before manufacturing. They tell you nothing about alkaloid content after heat processing. Finished product COAs tell you what's actually in the gummy you're consuming.

Quality gummy COAs should report mitragynine content per gummy unit — not just as a percentage by weight of the full batch. You need to know how much mitragynine is in each piece you consume. A batch-level percentage without per-unit calculation is insufficient dosing information for gummy format.

Standard quality kratom gummies deliver **5–15mg of mitragynine per gummy** depending on the product tier. Extract gummies can deliver **20–50mg per unit**. Know the number before you dose.
